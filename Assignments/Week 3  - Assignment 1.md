## Meta & Objectives

- **Why it matters:** A consistent look is what separates a portfolio that feels intentional from one that feels thrown together. The goal is to make a few visual decisions once so every page, case study, and project write-up feels connected.
    
- **The Brief:** Choose one or two free fonts, define a calm portfolio color palette, create a simple logo/favicon, and write a two-line style note that can be added to my Claude Project for future consistency.

### Font Choices

|Use|Font|Why|
|---|---|---|
|Headings|**Space Grotesk**|It feels modern and technical without looking too decorative. It works well for cybersecurity, AI, and engineering projects.|
|Body text|**Inter**|It is highly readable, clean, and professional for case studies, project pages, and technical explanations.|

**Font rule:** Use Space Grotesk only for headings, navigation, and short labels. Use Inter for paragraphs, tables, project descriptions, and long-form writing.

###  Color Palette

|Role|Hex code|Usage|
|---|---|---|
|Main color|`#2563EB`|Links, buttons, active states, important section markers|
|Text color|`#111827`|Main text, headings, body copy|
|Background|`#F9FAFB`|Main page background|
|Accent|`#22C55E`|Small highlights, status indicators, terminal-like details|

**Palette rule:** The blue is the main identity color. The green should be used rarely, only for small technical highlights, so the projects remain the focus.

###  Track Thread Summary

For my Week 3 identity kit, I chose a minimal technical direction for my portfolio.

My heading font is **Space Grotesk**, and my body font is **Inter**. The palette is intentionally tight: `#2563EB` as the main blue, `#111827` for near-black text, `#F9FAFB` for a soft near-white background, and `#22C55E` as a small signal-green accent.

The logo/favicon is a simple **Y** monogram inside a rounded square, inspired by a terminal cursor and network-node shape. The goal is a calm cybersecurity/AI research identity: technical, precise, and clean, without making the design compete with the actual work.

## AI Collaboration Log

- **Initial Draft / Thought Process:**
    
    - I wanted the identity to fit my actual work: cybersecurity, AI, RAG systems, CTFs, and research projects.
        
    - I did not want something too colorful, trendy, or “startup flashy,” because the portfolio should make the projects look serious and credible.
        
- **The Friction Points:**
    
    - The main challenge was choosing a palette that feels technical without looking aggressive or dark-only.
        
    - Another friction point was keeping the logo simple enough to work as a favicon instead of overdesigning it.
        
- **Refinements Made:**
    
    - I limited the kit to two fonts only: one heading font and one body font.
        
    - I kept the palette to four colors.
        
    - I chose a simple monogram instead of a complex symbol so it can scale down cleanly.
        
    - I wrote the Claude Project style note as an instruction that can guide future portfolio pages and case studies.
